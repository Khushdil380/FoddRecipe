Pav Bhaji

Category: Street Food | Cuisine: Indian

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 40 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 1 cup finely chopped capsicum
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 3 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp pav bhaji masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Salt to taste
  • 8 pav (bread rolls)
  • Extra butter for toasting pav

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Heat butter and oil in a large pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Step 2: Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  3. Step 3: Add capsicum, green peas, and cauliflower. Cook for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Step 4: Add mashed potatoes, pav bhaji masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Step 5: Add about 1 cup of water and mash the mixture with a masher to a smooth consistency. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Step 6: Add lemon juice and garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  7. Step 7: Heat a griddle with butter. Slice the pav horizontally and toast them until golden on both sides.
  8. Step 8: Serve the hot bhaji with buttered pav, chopped onions, and a wedge of lemon.

Tips & Tricks

  • Add a dollop of butter on top of the bhaji while serving for a richer taste.
  • Ensure the vegetables are cooked well and mashed to achieve a smooth texture.
  • Serve with finely chopped onions and lemon wedges for a tangy twist.
