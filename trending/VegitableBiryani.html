Vegetable Biryani

Category: Main Course | Cuisine: Indian

Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time: 40 minutes

Total Time: 55 minutes

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1 1/2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, potatoes, etc.)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/4 tsp saffron strands (optional)
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 1/2 cups water (for cooking rice)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it for 30 minutes. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. Step 2: Heat oil and ghee in a large pan or pressure cooker. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Remove half of the onions for garnishing later.
  3. Step 3: Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  4. Step 4: Add the mixed vegetables, biryani masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook the vegetables for about 5 minutes.
  5. Step 5: Add yogurt and stir well to coat the vegetables. Let it cook for another 3-4 minutes until the yogurt is absorbed and the oil separates.
  6. Step 6: In a separate large pot, bring 2 1/2 cups of water to a boil. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70% done. Drain excess water and set aside.
  7. Step 7: Layer the cooked vegetables and partially cooked rice in the same pan. Sprinkle saffron (if using), mint leaves, coriander leaves, and the reserved fried onions on top.
  8. Step 8: Cover and cook on low heat (or cook for 5-10 minutes in a pressure cooker on low flame) to let the flavors blend and rice fully cook.
  9. Step 9: Gently fluff the rice, mix the layers, and serve hot with raita or pickle.

Tips & Tricks

  • For a richer flavor, add whole garam masala (cardamom, cinnamon, cloves) while frying the onions.
  • To enhance the aroma, add a few drops of rose water or kewra water to the biryani before serving.
  • Ensure the rice is not overcooked to retain the fluffy texture of the biryani.
