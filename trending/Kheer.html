Kheer

Category: Desserts | Cuisine: Indian

Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 40 minutes

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped cashews
  • 1/4 cup chopped almonds
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tbsp rose water (optional)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  2. Step 2: In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat ghee on medium heat. Add cashews, almonds, and raisins. Fry them until golden brown and set aside.
  3. Step 3: In the same pan, add the rinsed rice and sauté for a minute in the ghee.
  4. Step 4: Add milk to the pan and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally to prevent the milk from burning. Cook for about 20 minutes or until the rice is soft and the milk thickens.
  5. Step 5: Once the rice is cooked, add sugar and cardamom powder. Stir until the sugar dissolves and the mixture thickens further.
  6. Step 6: Add the fried nuts and raisins back into the kheer. Stir well.
  7. Step 7: Add saffron strands and rose water (if using) for added flavor. Stir well and cook for another 2 minutes.
  8. Step 8: Remove from heat and let the kheer cool slightly. You can serve it warm or refrigerate it to serve chilled.

Tips & Tricks

  • If you prefer a creamier kheer, add some condensed milk along with regular milk.
  • For a vegan version, use coconut milk or almond milk instead of regular milk.
  • If you want a richer flavor, add a little ghee when serving and garnish with more nuts.
