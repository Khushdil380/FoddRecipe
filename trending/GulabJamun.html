Gulab Jamun

Category: Desserts | Cuisine: Indian

Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 40 minutes

Servings: 6-8

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 cup khoya (reduced milk)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • Milk as required
  • Ghee or oil for deep frying
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • A few saffron strands (optional)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: In a bowl, crumble the khoya and add all-purpose flour, baking soda, and ghee. Mix well.
  2. Step 2: Gradually add milk little by little to form a smooth dough. The dough should be soft but not sticky.
  3. Step 3: Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth balls, ensuring there are no cracks.
  4. Step 4: Heat ghee or oil in a pan for deep frying. The temperature should be low to medium. If the oil is too hot, the gulab jamuns will burn on the outside and remain raw inside.
  5. Step 5: Fry the balls in batches until golden brown, turning them gently for even cooking. Be careful not to overcrowd the pan.
  6. Step 6: Remove the gulab jamuns from the oil and place them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  7. Step 7: In a separate pan, make the sugar syrup by combining sugar, water, and cardamom powder. Bring to a boil and simmer for about 5-7 minutes until the syrup slightly thickens.
  8. Step 8: Add saffron strands to the syrup for color and fragrance (optional).
  9. Step 9: Once the syrup is ready, immerse the fried gulab jamuns into the warm syrup and let them soak for 30-45 minutes.
  10. Step 10: Serve warm or at room temperature. Enjoy your delicious Gulab Jamuns!

Tips & Tricks

  • Make sure to fry the gulab jamuns on low heat so they cook evenly.
  • If the dough is too sticky, add a little extra flour, but be careful not to make it too firm.
  • If you want a more fragrant syrup, you can add a few rose petals or a splash of rose water.
