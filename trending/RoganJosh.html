Rogan Josh

Category: Main Course | Cuisine: Indian (Kashmiri)

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 1 hour

Total Time: 1 hour 20 minutes

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton (preferably bone-in)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 3 medium onions, finely sliced
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 tsp fennel powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 green cardamoms
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Heat mustard oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Once hot, add ghee, cloves, cinnamon, and green cardamoms. Sauté for a few seconds until aromatic.
  2. Step 2: Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  3. Step 3: Add mutton pieces and sear them on high heat until they are browned evenly.
  4. Step 4: Lower the heat and add turmeric powder, Kashmiri red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and fennel powder. Mix well.
  5. Step 5: Whisk the yogurt and add it to the pan. Cook on low heat until the oil separates from the spices.
  6. Step 6: Add water (about 2 cups) and salt. Cover and let the mutton simmer on low heat for 45-50 minutes until tender.
  7. Step 7: Add garam masala and cardamom powder. Mix well and let it cook for another 5 minutes.
  8. Step 8: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or naan.

Tips & Tricks

  • For an authentic flavor, cook the dish on low heat for a longer time.
  • Use Kashmiri red chili powder for a vibrant color and mild heat.
  • Pair it with saffron-infused rice for a royal meal.
