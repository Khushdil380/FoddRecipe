Fish Curry

Category: Main Course | Cuisine: Indian

Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 45 minutes

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 500 grams fish (preferably boneless fillets like tilapia or catfish)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup coconut milk (optional, for a creamy texture)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter for a few seconds.
  2. Step 2: Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Step 3: Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Step 4: Add pureed tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the oil starts separating from the masala.
  5. Step 5: Add water and bring the curry to a simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Step 6: Gently add the fish fillets to the curry and cook for 8-10 minutes, ensuring the fish is tender and cooked through.
  7. Step 7: Stir in the coconut milk (optional) and let it cook for 2-3 more minutes until the curry reaches your desired consistency.
  8. Step 8: Add garam masala powder and lemon juice, stir gently.
  9. Step 9: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or naan.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use fresh fish for the best flavor. If you are using frozen fish, thaw it properly before cooking.
  • If you prefer a thicker curry, reduce the amount of water or let the curry simmer longer.
  • For extra richness, you can add a tablespoon of ghee (clarified butter) before serving.
