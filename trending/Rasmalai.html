Rasmalai

Category: Dessert | Cuisine: Indian

Preparation Time: 30 minutes

Cooking Time: 1 hour

Total Time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Servings: 6

Difficulty: Moderate

Ingredients

  • For Rasmalai Balls (Paneer):
    • 1 liter whole milk
    • 2 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 4 cups water
  • For Ras (Milk Syrup):
    • 1 liter full-fat milk
    • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust as per taste)
    • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
    • 10-12 saffron strands
    • 2 tbsp chopped pistachios and almonds

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Boil the milk in a pot. Once it boils, add lemon juice gradually and stir until the milk curdles. Strain using a muslin cloth to separate the paneer (chhena). Rinse it with cold water and press out excess moisture.
  2. Step 2: Knead the paneer for 8-10 minutes to make it smooth and soft. Divide it into small balls and flatten them slightly to form discs.
  3. Step 3: In a pressure cooker or deep pan, boil water with sugar. Add the paneer discs and cook for 8-10 minutes on medium flame. The discs will double in size.
  4. Step 4: Remove the cooked discs from the syrup and cool. Gently press to remove excess sugar syrup.
  5. Step 5: To prepare the milk syrup, boil full-fat milk in a thick-bottomed pan until it reduces to 2/3rd. Add sugar, cardamom powder, saffron, and half of the chopped nuts. Simmer for 5 minutes and remove from heat.
  6. Step 6: Add the prepared paneer discs into the milk syrup and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  7. Step 7: Serve chilled garnished with remaining pistachios and almonds.

Tips & Tricks

  • Ensure the paneer is smooth and soft for spongy rasmalai balls.
  • Do not overcrowd the syrup while cooking the paneer discs.
  • Let the rasmalai soak in milk syrup for a rich flavor.
