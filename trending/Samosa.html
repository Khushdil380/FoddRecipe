Samosa

Category: Appetizers | Cuisine: Indian

Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time: 25 minutes

Total Time: 40 minutes

Servings: 6

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 2 cups boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup boiled peas
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Spring roll or samosa pastry sheets
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Heat 2 tbsp oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Step 2: Add green chilies and grated ginger. Sauté for a minute.
  3. Step 3: Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Stir well and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Step 4: Add boiled potatoes and peas to the pan. Mash them together and cook for 5 minutes. Add salt and mix well.
  5. Step 5: Remove the filling from the pan and allow it to cool. Add fresh coriander leaves for garnish.
  6. Step 6: Once the filling cools, take a samosa pastry sheet and fold it into a cone shape. Stuff the cone with the prepared filling and seal the edges with a little water.
  7. Step 7: Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the samosas until golden brown and crispy.
  8. Step 8: Remove the samosas from the oil and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.

Tips & Tricks

  • You can add chopped carrots and beans to the filling for added texture and flavor.
  • If you prefer a healthier option, you can bake the samosas instead of frying them.
  • Make sure to seal the edges of the samosa properly to avoid the filling spilling out during frying.
