Daal Tadka

Category: Main Course | Cuisine: Indian

Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 40 minutes

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow split lentils (toor dal)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 cups water (for dal consistency)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Wash the toor dal thoroughly and cook it in a pressure cooker with 2-3 cups of water, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook for about 3-4 whistles or until the dal becomes soft and mushy.
  2. Step 2: Heat oil and ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Step 3: Add asafoetida (hing), chopped onions, and green chili. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  4. Step 4: Add chopped tomatoes, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil begins to separate.
  5. Step 5: Once the dal is cooked, add the prepared tadka (spiced mixture) to the dal and stir well. Adjust the consistency by adding water as needed.
  6. Step 6: Simmer for a few minutes and let the flavors meld together.
  7. Step 7: Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

Tips & Tricks

  • If you prefer a more flavorful tadka, add garlic and ginger along with the onions.
  • You can also add a squeeze of lemon juice before serving to enhance the flavor.
  • For a smoky flavor, temper the dal with a charcoal smoke at the end (optional).
