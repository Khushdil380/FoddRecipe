Paneer Butter Masala

Category: Main Course | Cuisine: Indian

Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 40 minutes

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 200 grams paneer (cubed)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/4 cup cream
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/4 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing
  • Water as required

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Heat butter in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Then add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Step 2: Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Step 3: Add pureed tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook the mixture on medium heat until the oil starts to separate from the masala (about 8-10 minutes).
  4. Step 4: Add cream, sugar, and garam masala. Stir well and cook for another 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  5. Step 5: Add paneer cubes to the gravy and gently mix. Cook for another 5 minutes so the paneer absorbs the flavor.
  6. Step 6: Crush kasuri methi between your palms and sprinkle it over the curry. Mix well.
  7. Step 7: If the gravy is too thick, add a little water to reach your desired consistency.
  8. Step 8: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Tips & Tricks

  • For a richer flavor, you can add a bit of saffron or a pinch of cardamom powder.
  • If you like a smoother texture, you can blend the tomato-onion gravy before adding the paneer cubes.
  • For a spicier version, increase the quantity of red chili powder or add a green chili while cooking the onions.
